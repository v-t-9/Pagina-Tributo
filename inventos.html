Nikola Tesla

Alternate current

In 1891, Tesla was given the US patent no. 447,921 for its alternating current generator.

For that time, the generator meant a more efficient way of providing electricity at a lower cost. The reason of this, is that it allows the possibility of transporting electricity over long distances.

Hidroelectric central

Tesla developed the 1st hydro-electric power station using the power generated by the Niagara Falls.

Radio

Marconi was not the inventor of the radio. Tesla already had a basic design for a radio system in 1892. However, it was not until 1898 that he patented and demonstrated his system at an exhibition in New York. He did it by using a remote-controlled boat that received radio waves through an antenna.

Nikola and Marconi had a legal battle over the patent. In 1943, 6 months after his death, the United States Supreme Court ruled in favor of Tesla, giving him the patents related to the invention and, declaring Marconi's patents invalid.

Tesla Coil

In 1891, the inventor registered an electrical transformer known as the Tesla coil. His idea was to transmit electricity without the need for cables.

Nowadays, not only this type of coils are used in high tension towers or in scientific laboratories but also, in the show business.

It is also possible to create homemade coils.

Neon sign

Tesla did not create neon or fluorecent lights but, he contributed to their improvement. He used existing technology to put together signs with words and designs.

Nowadays, these signs are used to identify places such as hotels and bars.

Shadowgraph or x-rays

X-rays were created by Whilhelm Rontgen in 1895. However, Tesla made an improvement on the invention by making the images sharper. This also led to his collaboration in discovering the effects of x-rays and how they can be used to improve human health.
